## Project Overview

**Leander High School** is one of Central Texas's most significant educational renovation projects at $200M, representing a complete transformation of the district's flagship facility. The comprehensive renovation addresses 50+ year old facilities while expanding capacity by 40% and creating spaces aligned with contemporary learning theory, workforce preparation, and community use. The project reimagines secondary education as a hub for academic, artistic, athletic, and civic engagement.

**Status**: Programming | **Phase**: Facility Needs Assessment | **Team Lead**: Zhun Jiao, Architect

## Project Scope

The renovation addresses the entire 520,000 sq ft campus with strategic new construction:

**Renovation Components**
- Main academic building (core renovation, upgraded systems)
- Athletics facilities (gymnasium, natatorium, training rooms)
- Auxiliary spaces (cafeteria, library, administration)
- Campus infrastructure (roads, utilities, parking)

**New Construction**
- Advanced STEM building (90,000 sq ft)
- Performing arts complex (50,000 sq ft)
- Innovation & maker spaces (25,000 sq ft)
- Outdoor learning terraces and amphitheater
- Expanded parking and drop-off zones

## Design Vision

The renovation transforms Leander High into a **21st-century learning destination**:

- **Academic Excellence**: Science/engineering labs, maker spaces, collaborative learning commons
- **Arts & Culture**: Professional-grade theater, recital halls, visual arts studios
- **Athletic & Wellness**: Competition and practice facilities, fitness centers, mind-body spaces
- **Community Hub**: Extended-hours programming, adult education, evening/weekend events
- **Environmental Stewardship**: Net-zero energy target, water independence, carbon neutrality
- **Accessibility & Inclusion**: Universal design, multilingual signage, culturally responsive spaces

## Sustainability Commitment

The project pursues **net-zero energy and water independence**:

- **Energy**: On-site solar (500 kW), geothermal systems, LED throughout, energy storage
- **Water**: Rainwater harvesting, wastewater treatment, drought landscape
- **Materials**: Sustainable procurement, embodied carbon accounting, construction waste diversion
- **Resilience**: Passive survivability, backup power, microgrids
- **Health**: Daylighting, natural ventilation, non-toxic materials, indoor environmental quality

## Educational Program

The spaces support diverse learning modalities:

- Flexible classrooms (movable walls, tech-enabled)
- Project-based learning labs
- Interdisciplinary collaboration zones
- Maker/fabrication spaces
- Career pathway facilities (healthcare, IT, skilled trades)
- Advanced learning technologies (VR, robotics, simulation)
