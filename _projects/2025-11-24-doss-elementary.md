## Project Overview

Doss Elementary School is an on-site replacement project for Austin Independent School District. The new 120,000 sq ft facility will serve 650 students (K-8) with modern, flexible learning spaces designed for 21st-century education and community engagement.

## Educational Design Concept

- **Learning Commons**: Open, flexible spaces replacing traditional corridors
- **STEM Labs**: Dedicated maker spaces, computer labs, science facilities
- **Multipurpose**: Gymnasium, auditorium, catering kitchen for community events
- **Library Commons**: Research, digital media, and social gathering hub
- **Outdoor Learning**: Native landscape gardens, outdoor classroom areas, athletic fields
- **Security & Safety**: Modern access control, secure perimeter, emergency protocols

## Building Sustainability

- **Net-zero energy target** via solar arrays and efficient HVAC systems
- **Rainwater capture** for landscape irrigation and toilet flushing
- **Daylighting strategy** reducing artificial lighting 30% year-round

## Community Impact

- **650 students** (K-8) with modern learning environment
- **Extended hours programming** for after-school and summer activities
- **Community facilities** available for evening/weekend events
- **Local hiring**: 60% of construction workforce from Austin area
- **Projected opening**: Fall 2026

## Project Budget & Timeline

- **Total project cost**: $72M ($600/sq ft)
- **Current phase**: Design Development
- **Permitting**: 6 months (Q1 2025)
- **Construction**: 24 months (Q2 2025 - Q1 2027)
- **Contingency**: 10% for site conditions and unforeseen needs
