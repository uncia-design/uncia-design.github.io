## Project Overview

**Atlantis 24-Hour Living District** is a visionary master plan for a mixed-use urban district emphasizing vibrant 24-hour activation, transit orientation, and diverse housing. The 25-acre district envisions 1,200 residential units (mix of market-rate and affordable), 200,000 sq ft of retail/dining/entertainment, offices, hotels, and extensive public spaces. The design prioritizes walkability, cultural vitality, sustainability, and community over automobilesâ€”creating a live-work-play destination.

**Status**: Master Planning | **Phase**: Design Framework Development | **Team Lead**: Zhun Jiao, Architect

## Master Plan Vision

The district creates urban vitality through mixed-use integration:

**Land Use Mix**
- Residential: 1,200 units (35-40 stories, diverse unit types)
- Retail & Dining: 200,000 sf (ground-floor activation)
- Office: 150,000 sf (creative industries, startups)
- Hotel: 250-room boutique hotel
- Entertainment/Culture: Theater, music venues, galleries, event spaces
- Public Space: 8+ acres parks, plazas, and gathering areas

**Residential Diversity**
- Market-rate apartments and condos
- Affordable housing (30% of units at 60-120% AMI)
- Live/work lofts for creative professionals
- Senior housing component
- Accessible design throughout

## 24-Hour Activation Strategy

The district maintains vibrancy across all times:

- **Morning**: Coffee shops, breakfast venues, early-hour fitness
- **Daytime**: Offices, shopping, dining, parks, cultural attractions
- **Evening**: Restaurants, bars, entertainment, music venues
- **Late Night**: 24-hour dining, night markets, entertainment venues, clubs
- **Overnight**: Residential population, 24-hour services, security presence

**Activation Mechanisms**
- Diverse mix of uses ensures round-the-clock activity
- Ground-floor transparency and inviting storefronts
- Programming: Markets, festivals, performances throughout year
- Event venues with 500-2,000 capacity for community gatherings
- Active transportation (pedestrians, cyclists) at all hours

## Transit & Connectivity

The district prioritizes public transportation and walkability:

- **Transit Hub**: Multi-modal station (bus, train, bike-share, car-share)
- **Pedestrian Network**: Traffic-free areas, wide sidewalks, climate-controlled passages
- **Bike Infrastructure**: Protected lanes, bike parking (1,000+ spaces), bike-share station
- **Car-Free Living**: 20% of residents car-free; shared vehicle access for others
- **Microhubs**: Last-mile delivery consolidation, package lockers

**Zero-Car Households**
- Public transit access (10-min walk to major routes)
- Ride-share integration
- Car-sharing fleet (150+ vehicles for occasional need)
- Bike-share and bike parking
- Walkable amenities (grocery, pharmacy, dining, entertainment)

## Public Realm Design

Extensive outdoor spaces encourage community gathering:

- **Central Plaza**: 2-acre multipurpose gathering space (markets, performances, events)
- **Linear Park**: 3-acre green corridor with native plantings and water features
- **Pocket Parks**: Four 0.5-acre neighborhood parks with varied programming
- **Outdoor Dining**: Streetside patios, food hall, vendor stalls
- **Performance Spaces**: Amphitheater, temporary stage areas
- **Play & Recreation**: Playground, fitness stations, ping-pong tables

## Affordability & Equity

Intentional strategy to ensure inclusive community:

- **Affordable Housing**: 30% of units (360 units) at 60-120% AMI
- **Community Land Trust**: Potential permanent affordability mechanism
- **Small Business Support**: Priority leasing for minority/women-owned businesses
- **Local Hiring**: 30% construction workforce from underrepresented communities
- **Community Benefits Agreement**: Resident input on uses and programming
- **Arts Integration**: Cultural programming, artist residencies, community galleries

## Sustainability Excellence

**Target: Carbon-Neutral District**

- **Energy**: District-level renewable generation (rooftop/canopy solar, geothermal), smart grid
- **Water**: Rainwater harvesting, greywater recycling, bioswales, zero stormwater discharge
- **Waste**: Composting, recycling, waste-to-energy systems, minimal landfill
- **Transportation**: EV charging (100+ Level 2, 30+ DC fast), transit-first design
- **Materials**: Circular economy principles, embodied carbon accounting
- **Biodiversity**: Native habitat restoration, green roofs (5+ acres), pollinator gardens
- **Resilience**: Microgrids, backup power, passive survivability
