## Project Overview

**C.D. Fulks Middle School** is a new Round Rock ISD facility designed to serve 900+ students with modern middle grades pedagogy. The design creates a supportive environment for young adolescents transitioning from elementary to secondary education, featuring flexible learning spaces, comprehensive student support services, and recreational facilities. The project demonstrates best practices in middle school design including small learning communities, mentoring spaces, and wellness integration.

**Status**: Design Phase | **Phase**: Design Development | **Team Lead**: Zhun Jiao, Architect

## Design Concept

The facility supports middle grades education philosophy:

- **Small Learning Communities**: Three academic pods enabling familiarity and personalized attention
- **Flexible Learning Spaces**: Open studios supporting diverse instructional approaches
- **Maker/Project Spaces**: Hands-on learning labs for STEM, arts, and design-thinking
- **Student Support Services**: Counseling, health services, alternative support spaces
- **Commons Areas**: Multiple informal gathering and eating spaces
- **Recreation Facilities**: Gymnasium, outdoor courts, green play spaces

## Academic Environment

Spaces designed for developmentally appropriate middle grades learning:

- **Science Laboratories**: Integrated chemistry, physics, biology, earth science labs
- **Technology Labs**: Computer science, digital media, engineering design
- **Arts Classrooms**: Visual arts studios, music/band rooms, performance space
- **Flexible Instruction**: Furniture and technology enabling collaborative and independent work
- **Literacy Support**: Dedicated reading/writing center with diverse text collections
- **Social-Emotional Learning**: Mindfulness spaces, peer mentoring zones, reflection areas

## Community Integration

The school serves as community gathering place:

- After-hours access for evening events and adult programming
- Community meeting rooms for civic and organizational use
- Outdoor amphitheater and gathering spaces
- Sports facilities available to broader community
- Cultural programming and student performances

## Sustainability & Wellness

**LEED Gold Certification** with emphasis on healthy environments:

- Daylighting and natural ventilation in 80% of spaces
- Indoor air quality: High-efficiency filtration, low-VOC materials
- Thermal comfort: Operable windows, radiant systems, natural cooling
- Water conservation: Low-flow fixtures, rainwater management
- Energy: Solar-ready infrastructure, efficient HVAC, LED lighting
- Outdoor spaces: Native plantings, water features, nature connection
